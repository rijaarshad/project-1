Achari Paneer Recipe

Achari Paneer is a flavorful and spicy Indian dish made with paneer cubes cooked in a tangy and aromatic achar (pickle) based gravy. This recipe is perfect for anyone who loves bold and unique flavors!

Ingredients

  • Homemade Cottage Cheese (Paneer)
  • Taza doodh (Fresh milk) - 2 litre
  • Lemon juice - 3 tbs
  • Water - 3 tbs
  • Cooking oil - 1-2 tbs
  • Pyaz (Onion) cubes - 1 medium
  • Tamatar (Tomatoes) cubes - 2 medium
  • Shimla mirch (Capsicum) cubes - 1 medium
  • Adrak lehsan paste (Ginger garlic paste) - 1 tbs
  • Achar gosht masala - 3-4 tbs
  • Kashmiri lal mirch powder - 1 tsp
  • Dahi (Yogurt) whisked - ½ Cup
  • Sugar - ½ tbs
  • Garam masala powder - ¼ tsp
  • Hara dhania (Fresh coriander) chopped - 2-3 tbs
  • Hari mirch (Green chilli) sliced - 1


Directions

  1. In a wok, add fresh milk and bring it to a boil. Turn off the flame, add water and lemon juice, stir gently, cover and let it rest for 5 minutes or until the milk curdles.
  2. Place a strainer and muslin cloth over a bowl, strain the curdled mixture to separate whey. Rinse thoroughly with water and squeeze out excess water. Enclose curd with muslin cloth and place heavy weight to drain excess moisture (30 minutes). Cottage cheese (paneer) is ready!
  3. Cut the prepared cheese into cubes and set aside.
  4. In a frying pan, add cooking oil, fry the paneer cubes on low flame until golden (4-5 minutes) and set aside.
  5. In a wok, sauté onions for a minute. Add tomatoes, capsicum, and cook for 1-2 minutes. Add water, blend the mixture to make a puree, and cook again with ginger garlic paste, achar gosht masala, kashmiri red chili powder, and oil.
  6. Add yogurt, mix well, and cook on low flame until oil separates (6-8 minutes). Add the fried paneer cubes, salt, sugar, garam masala, fresh coriander, and green chili. Simmer for 4-5 minutes.
  7. Garnish with green chili and coriander. Serve!
